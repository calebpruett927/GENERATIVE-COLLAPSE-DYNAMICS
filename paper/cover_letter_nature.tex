\opening{Dear Editors,}

I am writing to submit the manuscript entitled \textbf{``A single
algebraic diagnostic detects structurally identical phase transitions
from quarks to the cosmic horizon''} for consideration as an Article
in \emph{Nature Communications}.

\textbf{Summary.} We report an empirical finding: the ratio of
composite integrity to fidelity (IC/$F$)---the geometric mean of
normalized measurements divided by their arithmetic mean---computed
from a single algebraic kernel, produces structurally identical
phase-transition signatures across
systems spanning 61~orders of magnitude---from quark confinement
($10^{-15}$\,m) to dark-energy--dominated cosmology ($10^{26}$\,m).
At the quark--hadron boundary, IC/$F$ drops by a factor of~23 as
color confinement drives a single measurement channel to zero;
atomic structure subsequently restores coherence 35-fold through
emergent degrees of freedom. The heterogeneity gap
$\Delta = F - \text{IC}$ spikes at every phase boundary tested,
providing a single-number, scale-independent diagnostic for
structural fragility.

\textbf{Significance.} No existing cross-scale diagnostic has been
shown to yield the same phase-transition signature at both the hadron
and cosmological scales using identical parameters and identical
equations. The mathematical guarantee behind this diagnostic---three
algebraic identities holding exactly across all 406~physical objects
tested in 20~scientific domains (15{,}260~automated proofs, zero
violations)---ensures that the cross-scale comparisons are
structurally rigorous, not correlational. The integrity bound
IC~$\leq\,F$ corresponds to the classical weighted inequality of
arithmetic and geometric means; the contribution lies not in the
bound itself but in its systematic application as a
phase-transition diagnostic across scales from subatomic to
cosmological.

\textbf{Scope.} This work is interdisciplinary by construction:
the kernel operates identically across particle physics, atomic
physics, neuroscience, cosmology, and 16~additional domains.
We believe \emph{Nature Communications} is the ideal venue because
the result bridges multiple fields through a single quantitative
framework, and the paper includes three testable predictions
amenable to independent verification by domain specialists.
