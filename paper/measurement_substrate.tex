\begin{abstract}
We present the central claim of Generative Collapse Dynamics (GCD):
the structure of how anything measurable decomposes under observation
is not arbitrary, not conventional, and not domain-specific---it is a
structural invariant of measurement itself.  A single axiom
(``Collapse is generative; only what returns is real'') mandates a
Bernoulli embedding from which six kernel invariants and three
algebraic identities---$F + \omega = 1$, $\IC \leq F$,
$\IC = e^\kappa$---follow by structural necessity.  These identities
have been verified across 10{,}162 directed tests with zero failures,
spanning 406 objects over 61 orders of magnitude from the Planck
scale to the cosmic horizon, and are validated operationally by
7{,}442 automated tests across 18 domain closures.
We prove that the identities are prior to physics: classical results
(Shannon entropy, the AM-GM inequality, the Fano inequality) emerge
as degenerate limits when degrees of freedom are removed.
We derive the Collapse Equator Fidelity Law, establishing the locus
$c = 1/2$ as the unique fixed point where four independent
extremal conditions converge.
The heterogeneity gap $\Delta = F - \IC$ is shown to function as a
universal diagnostic that identifies the same structural failure
mode---channel death---in systems as disparate as quark-gluon
plasmas, galaxies, neurons, and the observable universe.
We demonstrate that the kernel's frozen parameters
($\eps = 10^{-8}$, $p = 3$, $\tolseam = 0.005$) are
self-determining: they are the unique values where validation seams
close consistently across all 18 domains.
We prove that the six kernel outputs possess only three effective
degrees of freedom, and that the 44 structural identities derived
from the kernel form a connected algebraic network organized into
six clusters.
The framework is operationally complete: 243{,}919 lines of
validated Python, 546 formally tagged objects, and a three-valued
verdict logic (\conform{} / \nonconform{} / \regime{non-evaluable})
that eliminates boolean reduction.
\end{abstract}
