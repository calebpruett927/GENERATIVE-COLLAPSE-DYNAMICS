What is GCD?

Generative Collapse Dynamics is a mathematical framework that measures what survives collapse and what returns. It begins from a single axiom — Axiom-0 — and derives a complete measurement protocol that works identically across 20 scientific domains, from particle physics to consciousness studies.

The framework is implemented as UMCP (Universal Measurement Contract Protocol), a reproducible validation system with 14,362 tests, 47 lemmas, 44 structural identities, and frozen parameters that are seam-derived — discovered by the mathematics, not chosen by convention.

The Kernel

Three Regimes

Regime classification is derived from gates on (ω, F, S, C) — never asserted.

Stable
ω < 0.038 ∧ F > 0.90 ∧ S < 0.15 ∧ C < 0.14
12.5% of Fisher space
Watch
0.038 ≤ ω < 0.30 (or Stable gates not all met)
24.4% of Fisher space
Collapse
ω ≥ 0.30
63.1% of Fisher space

Stability is rare — 87.5% of the manifold lies outside it. Return from collapse to stability is what the axiom measures.
