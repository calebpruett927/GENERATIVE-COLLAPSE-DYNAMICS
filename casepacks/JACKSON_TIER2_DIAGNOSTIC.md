## 7. What Jackson's Framework Gets Right (With Caveats)

1. **The directionality is correct.** Both v1 and v2 show monotonically improving fidelity from Level 0.5 to 9.0, which matches Jackson's narrative of recursive depth building toward "coherence." The kernel confirms this trajectory — fidelity does increase.

2. **The corruption zone (v2 Stage 11) is a real phenomenon.** The kernel's maximum heterogeneity gap and curvature spike at Stage 11 confirm that the corruption concept has structural content. "High recursion with low return fidelity" is not just a label — it produces measurable pathology.

3. **The non-monotonicity in v2 is structurally interesting.** The re-collapse valley (Stages 10-12) makes v2 more honest than v1. A framework that admits regression after progress is more realistic than one that only goes up.

## 8. What Jackson's Framework Gets Wrong

1. **ξ_J = 7.2 is not a regime boundary.** It is inside Collapse. The actual boundary is at 8.0.

2. **The fabricated regime labels.** Jackson uses "Fragmented," "Heterogeneous," "Coherent" — none of which are GCD regime labels. The real labels are Stable/Watch/Collapse, defined by frozen gates on (ω, F, S, C).

3. **The mathematics (ρ × δ = 7.2, φ² = 2.618).** These multiplicative relationships are not verified by any kernel computation. F + ω = 1, IC ≤ F, IC = exp(κ) are the only structural identities.

4. **No Stable regime is reached.** Jackson's highest levels are Watch, not Stable. The framework never achieves the simultaneous satisfaction of all four Stable gates.

5. **The discontinuity from 12.0 → 13.0 is unexplained.** Jackson's narrative doesn't account for how the system jumps from Collapse (ω = 0.356) to Watch (ω = 0.078) without intermediate stages.
