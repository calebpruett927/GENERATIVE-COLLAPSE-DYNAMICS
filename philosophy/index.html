Philosophy of Collapse

"The absurd does not liberate; it binds."

Paradoxum colendum est, non solvitur. — The paradox is to be cultivated, not solved.

GCD did not begin from philosophy. It began from measurement. But the structures that emerged from the axiom — collapse, return, the seam, the heterogeneity gap, geometric slaughter — turn out to be identical to structures independently discovered by four philosophical traditions. This page makes those convergences computationally precise.

These are not metaphors. Each mapping is a computable correspondence: the philosophical concept maps to a Tier-1 invariant, and the invariant can be calculated here on this page.

§1   Camus — The Absurd as Heterogeneity Gap

In The Myth of Sisyphus, Camus identifies the absurd as the gap between the human desire for meaning and the universe's indifference. In GCD, this gap has a precise name: Δ = F − IC, the heterogeneity gap.

The system "looks healthy" on average (F is fine) but is structurally fragile (IC is collapsed). Δ measures exactly this: the distance between how things appear (arithmetic mean) and how things cohere (geometric mean).
