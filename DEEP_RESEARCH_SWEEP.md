## 3. Cross-Domain Patterns NOT Mentioned in Existing Documents

### 3.1 The Weight-Heterogeneity Paradox

Finance and security use non-equal weights. Physics domains use equal weights. This creates a **structural asymmetry in what Δ = F − IC means across domains**.

With equal weights: Δ measures pure channel heterogeneity — the gap between arithmetic and geometric means with identical coefficients.

With non-equal weights: Δ conflates two sources of variation — channel heterogeneity AND weight heterogeneity. A perfectly uniform trace (all cᵢ = c) still yields Δ > 0 if the weights are non-equal, because F = c exactly but IC = exp(Σ wᵢ ln c) = c only when weights are equal and sum to 1. Wait — IC = exp(Σ wᵢ ln c) = exp(ln(c)·Σwᵢ) = c when Σwᵢ = 1, so for **uniform traces** the identity IC = F still holds regardless of weights.

But the *sensitivity* of Δ to channel perturbations changes with weight distribution. A near-ε channel with weight 0.40 (security's trust channel) is more devastating to IC than a near-ε channel with weight 0.125 (SM's equal channel). This means **the same physical channel death produces different Δ magnitudes depending on domain weight structure**. Neither CROSS_DOMAIN_REALIZATIONS.md nor SYNTHESIS_REPORT.md discusses this.

**Implication**: Cross-domain IC/F comparisons between equal-weight and non-equal-weight domains are not directly commensurable. The "Scale Ladder" in CROSS_DOMAIN_REALIZATIONS.md compares IC/F across scales but all physics domains use equal weights. Adding finance or security to the ladder would require either weight normalization or explicit acknowledgment that the comparison is apples-to-oranges.

### 4.3 The Helium Paradox

## 6. epistemic_weld.py → Domain Closure Implications

### 6.1 Observation Cost Budget

The positional illusion severity = Γ(ω)/R where R is the available return credit. When severity ≥ 1.0, the observation consumes the entire budget.

For each domain, this implies a **maximum observation frequency**: you cannot measure more often than the budget allows.
