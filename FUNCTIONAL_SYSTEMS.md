> *UMCP metitur; RCFT explorat; ULRC sensum servat.*
> "UMCP measures; RCFT explores; ULRC preserves language."

This is the **functional-axis keystone** — the companion to [TIER_SYSTEM.md](TIER_SYSTEM.md)
(the authority axis). Together they are the two orthogonal architectures generated by
**Generative Collapse Dynamics**. The constitution above them — object *Reditus* → field
*Structura Reditus* → theory GCD — and the matrix where the axes meet are in

## The two axes — separation of powers

GCD generates two orthogonal axes. They are not one hierarchy.

- **Authority axis** (the tiers, [TIER_SYSTEM.md](TIER_SYSTEM.md)) governs *what may be
  changed*: Tier-1 (law), Tier-0 (execution), Tier-2 (admission).
- **Functional axis** (this document) governs *what is being done*: UMCP (measures),
  RCFT (explores), ULRC (preserves language).

A functional system **operates across all three tiers** but is **never itself a tier**.
This is the separation of powers: no system may both perform the work and rewrite the law
under which the work is judged.

## UMCP — Measurement and Evaluation

**Definition.** UMCP is the contract-first **measurement protocol** of GCD. It formalizes
how an object becomes evaluable under declared structure, and it operationalizes *Reditus*
by requiring every evaluable object to pass through declared structure before receiving
credit.

**Governing question.** *What must be declared before judgment becomes admissible?*

**Governing discipline.** *Do not judge first. Freeze first.* — the operational form of
Axiom-0: appearance is insufficient; repetition is not return; stance is not valid until
the rule-set has been declared.

**It requires:** object declaration · frozen contract · bounded trace · declared closures ·
missingness policy · typed return · ledger reconciliation · derived stance. Every evaluation
runs the spine: **Contract → Canon → Closures → Integrity Ledger → Stance** (stance read
last).

**Tier relation.** UMCP is **Tier-0 dominant** (its executable center is protocol) but
**cross-tier**: it preserves Tier-1 meanings, executes Tier-0 procedures, and evaluates
Tier-2 domain objects through declared closures. **UMCP is not Tier-0 itself, and not the
whole framework.**

**Final burden.** Make return **evaluable**.

[contracts/](contracts) (frozen contracts), and the C99 core [src/umcp_c/](src/umcp_c).

## RCFT — Discovery and Exploration

**Definition.** RCFT is the **discovery engine** of GCD — the disciplined environment in
which candidate structures, mappings, diagnostics, and closure paths are explored under the
logic of collapse and return **before they are frozen**.

**Governing question.** *What new structures emerge when collapse–return is explored
recursively?*

**Governing discipline.** *Explore freely. Freeze carefully.* — it must allow broad
exploration (new closure families cannot be discovered if only already-frozen structures are
permitted), while preserving the distinction between exploration and canon.

**The third position.** RCFT prevents two opposite failures: **premature canonization**
(treating a candidate as already validated) and **premature rejection** (discarding a
candidate because it is not yet frozen). It holds candidates as *exploratory but not yet
canonical* until they pass contract, closure, ledger, and stance.

**Tier relation.** RCFT **operates across all tiers** — it can explore candidate Tier-1
structures, candidate Tier-0 protocol extensions, and candidate Tier-2 closures. It **often
generates Tier-2 closures** (the closure family in [closures/rcft/](closures/rcft) and
[docs/rcft_theory.md](docs/rcft_theory.md) is exactly that), **but RCFT itself is not
Tier-2 and not a tier.** Exploration is not authority: a structure explored in RCFT is not
canonical merely because it is generative, useful, or recurrent.

**Final burden.** Make discovery **traceable**.

**Home.** [docs/rcft_theory.md](docs/rcft_theory.md) (the Tier-2 closure family it
develops), [closures/rcft/](closures/rcft), [canon/rcft_anchors.yaml](canon/rcft_anchors.yaml),
[paper/rcft_second_edition.tex](paper/rcft_second_edition.tex).

## ULRC — Language and Translation

**Definition.** ULRC is the **language and translation system** of GCD. It preserves the
structural meaning of collapse, return, contract, closure, trace, missingness, ledger,
stance, integrity, seam, weld, admissibility, and domain readback across the whole corpus.

**Governing question.** *How is collapse–return communicated across domains without losing
structural meaning?*

**Governing discipline.** *Translate without drift.* — it does not require every domain to
speak identically; it requires translation to preserve the **structural burden** of each
term. A word that keeps its spelling but loses its burden has *repeated*, not *returned*.

**Language as return.** ULRC is the linguistic form of *Reditus*: a term must return through
translation, critique, and formalization with its operative burden intact. The formal
translation rule: a translation `M(T)` of a structural term `T` is admissible only when
`Burden(M(T)) = Burden(T)`.

**Cross-domain translation (the Rosetta).** ULRC maps the five words (Drift, Fidelity,
Roughness, Return, Integrity) across lenses — epistemology, ontology, phenomenology,
history, policy, semiotics — so domains can read each other without semantic drift.
Integrity is never asserted in the Rosetta; it is read from the reconciled ledger.

**Tier relation.** ULRC is **cross-tier** — it stabilizes Tier-1 vocabulary, names Tier-0
protocol operations, and translates Tier-2 domain readbacks. **ULRC is not a tier and not a
domain closure**; it is the grammar that prevents semantic drift across the whole
architecture.

**Final burden.** Make meaning **return**.

## Functional non-equivalence — no system replaces another

The systems are coordinated but **non-equivalent**, and the strength of the axis lies in the
separation:

- **UMCP** evaluates; it does not freely explore.
- **RCFT** explores; it does not derive final stance.
- **ULRC** preserves language; it does not freeze contracts, compute kernels, reconcile
  ledgers, authorize closures, or canonize discoveries.

> **No Replacement Rule.** No functional system replaces another. UMCP without RCFT risks
> measurement without discovery; RCFT without UMCP risks exploration without audit; ULRC
> replacing either risks language being mistaken for proof.

> **No Tier Substitution Rule.** No functional system may be treated as a tier, and no tier
> may be treated as a functional system. The systems operate across the tiers; they do not
> become tiers by doing so.

## Interlock — the maturity path

Responsible expansion runs through all three systems in order:

```
RCFT  → candidate structure          (explore freely; freeze carefully)
ULRC  → burden-preserving language    (translate without drift)
UMCP  → contract-first evaluation     (do not judge first; freeze first)
Tier-2 → domain readback              (translate; never redefine the kernel)
```

A candidate may originate in RCFT, have its language disciplined by ULRC, then be tested for
evaluability by UMCP. If it becomes a domain pathway, it enters through a declared Tier-2
closure. No final credit is granted until the object becomes evaluable under declared
structure.

## Failure conditions

| System | Signature failure |
|--------|-------------------|
| **UMCP** | Judgment before freeze — stance depending on hidden judgment, moving thresholds, silent repair, or undeclared closure. |
| **RCFT** | Exploration as authority — treating exploration as proof, canonizing candidates before freeze, or letting a Tier-2 candidate redefine Tier-1. |
| **ULRC** | Repetition without return of meaning — return reduced to repetition, closure to conclusion, stance to opinion, integrity asserted before the ledger. |
| **Shared** | **Role collapse** — a functional system mistaken for a tier, a tier for a system, a closure for the kernel, a diagnostic for a gate, or a candidate for canon. |
